## The Situation in 30 Seconds

CSPD launched a major pedagogical transformation — the Uplift framework — across 80 schools in August 2025, just as it locked in ~$19m/year in new salary costs via the EA and recorded a -$20.6m operating deficit. The person brought in to deliver Uplift, Patrick Ellis (EGM Learning Outcomes, in post since September 2025), ran the exact same programme at Catholic Education Canberra-Goulburn. He knows what worked there. He knows what's missing here. The missing pieces — structured curriculum resources, a formative assessment analytics layer, and a teacher workload relief programme — are the same ones CECG had to build, and they are buildable within 12 months. The first post-Uplift NAPLAN cycle is 2026. That deadline is not soft.

## What Their Peers Are Doing

**Catholic Education Canberra-Goulburn (CECG) — the direct ancestor of Uplift**
Launched the Catalyst explicit instruction programme in 2020. Built 5,400+ structured curriculum resources for primary English and Mathematics with Ochre Education. Commissioned Deloitte Access Economics as external evaluator from Day 1. Result after three years: 75% reading proficiency vs 66% national average. 13 of the top 20 ACT schools "making the most difference" are CECG schools. Teachers save up to 6 hours/week. 4 in 5 teachers recommend the approach. Patrick Ellis was the Education Lead at CECG before joining CSPD.

**Melbourne Archdiocese Catholic Schools (MACS) — one year ahead on the same transformation**
Launched Vision for Instruction in February 2024 across 291 primary schools. Early-adopter schools showed NAPLAN numeracy improvements of up to 46 percentage points. MACS's Chief of Education attributed the results directly to explicit instruction. In July 2025, MACS also announced a SAP S/4HANA + SAP SuccessFactors ERP transformation to consolidate HR, finance, and operations across ~300 schools — explicitly framed as "paving the way to adopt generative AI."

**Brisbane Catholic Education (BCE) — the teacher workload benchmark**
Deployed Microsoft 365 Copilot to 12,500 educators — the largest K-12 generative AI rollout globally. Custom Copilot Studio solution grounded in Catholic values (not open generative AI). Trial at St Francis College Crestmead: 9.3 hours saved per teacher per week. Eight pilot schools grew NAPLAN scores from 490 to 555; Year 3 improved 7.22% while the state average fell 0.57%. BCE's AI is teacher-facing, values-grounded, and within a governed environment — it is not in contradiction with a "domed" policy.

**Catholic Education Western Australia (CEWA) — the analytics benchmark**
Built LEADing Lights: a single Microsoft cloud platform integrating student performance, attendance, wellbeing, HR, and finance across 164 schools. Principals see real-time Power BI dashboards benchmarking their school against system averages and NAPLAN/WACE data. The goal is moving from "big picture analytics" to "moment in time, individual person recommendations." The CSNSW Azure Databricks platform already serves all 11 NSW dioceses including CSPD — this infrastructure exists, CSPD is connected to it.

## The Three Things Keeping Them Up at Night

**1. Will Uplift actually land across 80 schools before the 2026 NAPLAN cycle?**

CSPD's 2023 Annual Report is unusually candid: there is "an imperative to increase the transition of students from Strong to Exceeding, particularly in Numeracy" (p.22). Three of that year's stated priorities were direct responses to this gap. Uplift was launched in August 2025 with no confirmed curriculum resource library, no external evaluation partner, and the accountable EGM only in post in September. The 2023 engagement data (69% score, 53% response rate, self-identified "communication gaps") signals that system-wide adoption is not automatic. CECG took three years to get measurable results — and they had the resource library from the beginning. Without it, CSPD is asking 3,760 teachers to change their practice while still building all the tools themselves.

*The loss: without a curriculum resource base and evaluation baseline before the 2026 NAPLAN cycle, CSPD will have no way to demonstrate Uplift's impact to the Board, parents, or AERO — and peers will already be publishing data.*

**2. Can we hold our teachers when every system in NSW is competing for the same people?**

The Inter-Diocesan Teacher Workforce Working Group met five times in 2024. The teacher shortage is classified as CSPD's highest-probability external risk. CSPD added 190 new teachers in 2023 while growing enrolments by 1.8% in 2024 — the demand keeps rising. CSPD also requires RE accreditation on top of standard NESA registration, narrowing the eligible candidate pool against public and independent school competitors who face no such parallel requirement. Uplift's rollout is adding professional learning obligations to already-stretched teachers. BCE's 9.3 hours/week saving per teacher is now the public benchmark. The question "what are you doing about teacher workload?" is coming from every principal.

*The loss: teacher attrition during the Uplift rollout period would be doubly costly — the salary costs are already locked in at +3%/year under the EA, while the pedagogical programme depends on sustained teacher engagement. A disengaged or depleted workforce is the single biggest execution risk to the strategy.*

**3. Will the operating deficit compound to something unmanageable?**

The 2023 result was -$20.6m on $857.2m revenue. Salaries are 71.6% of expenditure and are now legally committed to grow at 3% per annum through 2026 — approximately $19m/year in additional cost, roughly matching the entire current deficit. Capital outflows ($85.9m in 2023) exceed capital revenues ($36.2m) by $49.7m annually. Revenue is 80.7% government-sourced with the post-2029 Better and Fairer Schools Agreement still unresolved. The Sustainability pillar of the Five-Year Plan is the stated response — but no efficiency targets or back-office integration plans are in the public record. At CSPD's scale, fragmented finance and HR systems across 80+ schools are almost certainly generating material avoidable overhead that MACS and CEWA have both moved to address.

*The loss: each year the Sustainability pillar remains an aspiration rather than a programme, the structural deficit compounds. At some point it constrains the capital pipeline and forces a choice between strategic investment and financial stability.*

This directly addresses the two gaps that distinguish CECG's successful Catalyst from a framework that sits in a policy document: the resource infrastructure that relieves teacher planning load, and the evaluation credential that makes results legible to external stakeholders.

Patrick Ellis has built this before. He knows what it costs to not have it. This is the first conversation.

**Recommendation 2: Principal Analytics Layer on the Canvas and CSNSW Infrastructure**
*For Frances Waterford, EGM IT — and Patrick Ellis, EGM Learning Outcomes*

A diagnostic and build engagement that surfaces the Canvas formative assessment data already being collected into principal-level dashboards: school-vs-system benchmarks, proficiency progression tracking, Uplift adoption signals. This does not require a new data platform — CSNSW's Azure Databricks infrastructure already serves CSPD. The build is incremental, not greenfield. CEWA's LEADing Lights is the benchmark model. The hard deadline is the 2026 NAPLAN cycle: without this layer in place, CSPD cannot interpret or act on system-level results. Scoped as a Phase 1 diagnostic (what does Canvas currently export? what does CSNSW Azure already provide? where is the gap?) followed by a principal dashboard design and Uplift tracking layer.

This is a discussion Frances Waterford will immediately frame in ROI terms — the CECG and CEWA comparators give you the numbers.

**Recommendation 3: AI-Assisted Teacher Workload Programme Within the Domed Policy**
*For Frances Waterford and Daniel Lynch, EGM People & Culture*

A teacher-facing AI pilot — modelled on BCE's St Francis College trial — that operates entirely within CSPD's existing "domed" governance model. Not student-facing open AI. Not a challenge to the safeguarding policy. A controlled pilot measuring hours saved in administrative tasks, Uplift lesson planning adaptation, and formative assessment feedback generation. Deliverables: approved tool selection (M365 Copilot or a Catholic-values-grounded equivalent), pilot cohort design, data collection protocol, and a governance framework extending "domed" to teacher-facing tools. BCE's Catholic-values-grounded Copilot Studio demonstrates the model is not in contradiction with CSPD's mission.

This is a workforce retention investment framed for Daniel Lynch, and a technology capability investment framed for Frances Waterford. It needs both.
